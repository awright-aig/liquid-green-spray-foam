FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about why Liquid Green spray foam outperforms conventional insulation.

Is spray foam insulation better than fiberglass?

Yes. Spray foam outperforms fiberglass on every important measure: it seals air leaks (fiberglass doesn't), it doesn't settle or sag over time, it has higher R-value per inch, and it acts as both insulation and air barrier in one application.

Is spray foam insulation better than cellulose?

Yes. Liquid Green spray foam far exceeds cellulose in moisture resistance, doesn't provide a food source for pests, and won't settle. Cellulose can lose R-value as it compacts over the years; spray foam does not.

Does spray foam help with soundproofing?

Yes. Spray foam significantly reduces indoor noise pollution. Open cell spray foam is particularly effective at sound dampening, making it a great choice for interior walls, media rooms, and bedrooms near noisy areas.

Will spray foam lower my energy bills?

Yes. Because spray foam insulates and seals in one application, it stops drafts, cold spots, and air leakage — the main causes of energy waste. Many homeowners see heating and cooling costs drop by up to 50% after installation.

Is spray foam insulation safe after it cures?

Yes. Once cured (within 24 hours), Liquid Green spray foam is inert and safe. It does not off-gas, supports healthier indoor air quality, and helps lower free-floating allergens by sealing the building envelope against outdoor pollutants.

Does spray foam insulation need to be replaced?

No. Properly installed spray foam is a permanent insulation solution. It does not settle, sag, compress, or lose R-value over time, which is one of the key advantages over fiberglass and cellulose.
